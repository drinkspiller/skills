# Copy Frameworks Catalog

Each framework is an actionable card: structure, when to use, and an example.
Select 2-3 per brief based on the objective.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)

**Structure**: Hook → expand → make them want it → tell them what to do

**Best for**: Direct response, landing pages, email

**Example**:

-   **A**: "You're mass-deleting emails again, aren't you?"
-   **I**: "The average professional spends 28% of their day on email."
-   **D**: "What if your inbox only showed what actually matters?"
-   **A**: "Try Spark free for 14 days."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solve)

**Structure**: Name the pain → twist the knife → offer relief

**Best for**: Direct response, problem-aware audiences, conversion copy

**Example**:

-   **P**: "Your CRM is a graveyard of dead leads."
-   **A**: "Every day you don't follow up, a competitor does."
-   **S**: "Autopilot pings cold leads before they forget you exist."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. BAB (Before → After → Bridge)

**Structure**: Current reality → desired reality → how to get there

**Best for**: Product launches, transformation products, case studies

**Example**:

-   **Before**: "Onboarding a new hire takes 3 weeks and a binder."
-   **After**: "What if they were productive by Tuesday?"
-   **Bridge**: "Ramp does onboarding in 48 hours. No binder required."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Feature-Benefit Flip

**Structure**: Take a feature → translate to human outcome → lead with outcome

**Best for**: Tech products, feature-heavy launches, spec sheets that need soul

**Example**:

-   **Feature**: "256-bit encryption"
-   **Benefit**: "Your data has a better lock than Fort Knox."
-   **Flip**: "Fort Knox-grade security. Without the gold vault."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Disruption (Break the pattern)

**Structure**: Say the thing nobody in the category says

**Best for**: Brand awareness, challenger brands, category disruption

**Example**:

-   Category norm: "The most advanced skincare technology..."
-   Disruption: "Your skin doesn't care about technology. It cares about
    moisture."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Provocation (Uncomfortable > Comfortable)

**Structure**: Make a claim that creates productive discomfort

**Best for**: Brand manifestos, rallying cries, bold positioning

**Example**:

-   Safe: "We believe in doing things differently."
-   Provocative: "Most productivity apps make you less productive. We counted."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Contrast Pair

**Structure**: [Desirable thing] + [Unexpected qualifier]

**Best for**: Taglines, headlines, social copy — anywhere brevity is king

**Examples**:

-   "Tastes like Saturday. Priced like Tuesday."
-   "Looks handmade. Ships overnight."
-   "Enterprise features. Startup price."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Story Spine

**Structure**: Once upon a time → Every day → Until one day → Because of that →
Ever since

**Best for**: Brand stories, longer-form copy, video scripts

**Example**:

-   "Every morning, you open 47 tabs. One day, you opened one. Because Notion
    put everything in one place. Now you close your laptop at 5."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. The Reversal

**Structure**: Start with the expected conclusion → reverse it

**Best for**: Social ads, headlines, attention-grabbing openers

**Example**:

-   Expected: "We built the fastest processor ever."
-   Reversal: "We didn't build a faster processor. We eliminated the reason you
    needed one."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. The Admission

**Structure**: Concede a weakness → reframe as a strength

**Best for**: Challenger brands, authenticity plays, trust-building

**Example**:

-   "We're not the cheapest. We're the one you won't have to replace."
-   "Our app is ugly. It's also the only one that works offline."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 11. The Specificity Play

**Structure**: Replace every vague word with a specific number, name, or detail

**Best for**: Landing pages, product pages, credibility-driven copy

**Example**:

-   Vague: "Thousands of happy customers"
-   Specific: "11,247 teams shipped faster last quarter."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 12. The Cultural Anchor

**Structure**: Reference something the audience already knows → attach your
product to it

**Best for**: Social copy, brand awareness, audiences with shared references

**Example**:

-   "The Spotify Wrapped of your finances. Except it's useful year-round."
-   "It's like Waze, but for your calendar."

**Warning**: Cultural references decay. Use evergreen anchors (Spotify, Google
Maps) over trending ones (memes, viral moments).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Framework Selection Matrix

Objective           | Primary Framework | Secondary        | Tertiary
------------------- | ----------------- | ---------------- | ----------------
Brand awareness     | Disruption        | Provocation      | Cultural Anchor
Direct response     | PAS               | AIDA             | Specificity Play
Product launch      | BAB               | Feature-Benefit  | Contrast Pair
Emotional resonance | Story Spine       | Admission        | Reversal
Repositioning       | Reversal          | Disruption       | Admission
Social / viral      | Contrast Pair     | Cultural Anchor  | Provocation
Trust-building      | Admission         | Specificity Play | BAB
