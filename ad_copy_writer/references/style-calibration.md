# Style Calibration Guide

Detailed rubric for evaluating ad copy quality. Use during Phase 4 (CRITIQUE).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Score Scale

Score | Level        | What it means
----- | ------------ | ------------------------------------------------
10    | Iconic       | Enters the cultural lexicon. Quoted for decades.
9     | Award-worthy | Stops you mid-scroll. You screenshot it.
8     | Excellent    | Sharp, memorable, clearly above category norms.
7     | Good         | Professional, effective, but not surprising.
6     | Competent    | Does the job. Forgettable by tomorrow.
5     | Generic      | Could be any brand. Interchangeable.
4     | Weak         | Confusing, cluttered, or off-tone.
3     | Bad          | Actively damages perception.
1-2   | Harmful      | Offensive, misleading, or legally risky.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Calibration Benchmarks

### Score 10 — Iconic

-   "Just Do It." (Nike) — 3 words. Universal. Lasted 35+ years.
-   "Think Different." (Apple) — Grammatically wrong on purpose. Unforgettable.
-   "Got Milk?" (California Milk Board) — Two words. Created a cultural moment.

Why they work: Radical simplicity. No features. No benefits. Pure attitude that
the audience adopts as identity.

### Score 9 — Award-worthy

-   "The best a man can get." (Gillette) — Elevates a razor to aspiration.
-   "Because you're worth it." (L'Oréal) — Makes the product secondary to the
    person.
-   "Belong anywhere." (Airbnb) — Reframes lodging as belonging.

Why they work: One clear idea, emotionally precise, impossible to confuse with a
competitor.

### Score 8 — Excellent

-   "Save money. Live better." (Walmart) — Direct, specific promise.
-   "What's in your wallet?" (Capital One) — Conversational, sticky.
-   "Eat Fresh." (Subway) — Two words. Clear differentiation.

Why they work: Clear value proposition, memorable phrasing, strong brand fit.
Not transcendent, but consistently effective.

### Score 7 — Good

-   "The Ultimate Driving Machine." (BMW) — Effective but relies on superlative.
-   "Finger Lickin' Good." (KFC) — Memorable but dated style.

Why they're 7 and not 8+: They work, but the technique is visible. You can see
the copywriter's hand.

### Score 5-6 — Generic / Competent

-   "Innovation for everyone." — Who said this? Could be 50 brands.
-   "Empowering your journey." — Means nothing specific.
-   "Solutions that work for you." — The "you" is doing no work.

Why they fail: No specificity, no surprise, no personality.

## Test Rubrics (detailed)

### Billboard Test (weight: 0.25)

Score | Criteria
----- | ----------------------------------------------------
9-10  | Understood at 65mph. One glance. Zero ambiguity.
7-8   | Understood quickly but benefits from a second read.
5-6   | Requires stopping to parse. Too many words or ideas.
3-4   | Confusing. Reader isn't sure what's being sold.
1-2   | Incomprehensible or misleading.

### Swap Test (weight: 0.25)

Score | Criteria
----- | ---------------------------------------------------------
9-10  | Only this brand could say this. Rooted in specific truth.
7-8   | Strong brand fit but 1-2 competitors could also claim it.
5-6   | Generic claim. "Innovation" / "quality" / "trust."
3-4   | Actively contradicts what the brand stands for.
1-2   | Feels like a competitor's ad.

### Bar Test (weight: 0.20)

Score | Criteria
----- | -------------------------------------------------------
9-10  | You'd quote it to a friend unprompted. It sounds human.
7-8   | Natural and conversational. Not stiff.
5-6   | Reads like marketing. Professional but impersonal.
3-4   | Corporate jargon. "Leverage" / "synergy" / "ecosystem."
1-2   | Embarrassing to say out loud.

### Delete Test (weight: 0.15)

Score | Criteria
----- | -----------------------------------------------------
9-10  | Every word is load-bearing. Remove one and it breaks.
7-8   | Tight. Maybe one word could go.
5-6   | 20-30% flab. Adverbs, filler phrases.
3-4   | Half the words are decoration.
1-2   | More filler than content.

### Overnight Test (weight: 0.15)

Score | Criteria
----- | ---------------------------------------------------------
9-10  | Stuck in your head. You'll think of it randomly tomorrow.
7-8   | Memorable if you see it again. Recognition, not recall.
5-6   | Forgotten within minutes of scrolling past.
3-4   | Indistinguishable from adjacent content.
1-2   | Actively forgettable. Mind rejects it.

## Tone Spectrum

Place every piece of copy on this spectrum and note where it lands:

```
← Formal ──────────────────────────────────────── Casual →
  Corporate   Professional   Conversational   Playful   Irreverent
```

```
← Rational ────────────────────────────────────── Emotional →
  Data-driven   Benefit-led   Aspiration   Feeling   Visceral
```

The target zone for most ad copy is **Conversational + Benefit-led** to
**Playful + Aspiration**. Move toward Irreverent/Visceral only when the brand
and audience clearly support it.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Common Score Traps

### The 7 Trap

Copy that scores 7 across all tests = mediocre everywhere. Better to score
9/9/6/6/7 (sharp in some areas) than 7/7/7/7/7 (flat).

**Fix**: Identify the weakest test. Over-index on the strongest to create a
spike.

### The Clever Trap

Copy that's witty but unclear. High Bar Test, low Billboard Test.

**Fix**: "If I have to explain the joke, it's not an ad — it's a riddle."

### The Safe Trap

Copy that's clear and professional but has zero personality. High Billboard, low
Bar, low Overnight.

**Fix**: Apply Provocation or Admission framework. Add a human voice.
