# Insight Mining & Tension Discovery

Great ideas originate from unspoken truths rather than unguided speculation.
This document outlines methodologies for discovering consumer insights, mapping
foundational tensions, and framing strategic opportunity spaces before ideation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. The Insight Mining Sequence

When executing Phase 2 (`INSIGHT`), follow this structured progression to
uncover high-signal opportunity areas.

### 1.1 Mark Pollard's Four Points

Map the foundational strategic territory across four interconnected pillars:

1.  **Problem**: The immediate friction, operational bottleneck, or unmet need
    experienced by the target audience.
2.  **Insight**: A surprising, unarticulated truth explaining *why* the problem
    persists.
3.  **Advantage**: The unique technological capability, architectural
    positioning, or creative perspective our solution provides.
4.  **Strategy**: The overarching approach applying the advantage directly
    against the mined insight.

### 1.2 Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Analysis

Identify the underlying motivation driving the audience's behavior. Determine
the specific functional, emotional, and social "job" the consumer or engineer
hires the product or architecture to accomplish.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Tension Spotting

Every impactful concept resolves a foundational tension. Examine the domain to
spot friction across three specific dimensions:

### 2.1 Cultural Tension

The divergence between societal expectations or accepted norms and actual
real-world human behavior (what people claim to value versus what they actually
do).

### 2.2 Category Tension

The discrepancy between standard industry conventions or marketing promises and
the reality of product delivery (where existing solutions within the domain
consistently disappoint users).

### 2.3 Human Tension

The internal cognitive friction between an individual's core desire and the
external or psychological obstacles blocking their progress.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Opportunity Framing

### 3.1 How-Might-We (HMW) Laddering

Transform mined tensions into actionable design challenges by formulating three
distinct HMW questions across varying abstraction tiers:

*   **Broad Tier**: Explores systemic or industry-wide paradigm shifts.
*   **Medium Tier**: Targets specific workflow redesigns or capability
    introductions.
*   **Narrow Tier**: Focuses on surgical component interactions or concrete
    performance optimizations.

### 3.2 Abstraction Laddering

Evaluate the generated HMW tiers and select the optimal rung on the abstraction
ladder. The chosen rung must be abstract enough to inspire multiple distinct
architectural approaches, yet concrete enough to provide verifiable
implementation criteria.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. The Canonical Insight Formula

Every mined insight must be distilled into a single, unambiguous sentence that
passes the resonance check ("does this articulate an obvious truth that the
audience has felt but never heard stated directly?").

**Formula**:

> `[Target Audience] wants [Desire], but [Obstacle stands in the way], because
> [Root Cause].`
