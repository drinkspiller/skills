# Copywriting devices

Actionable rhetorical devices drawn from the top 500 award-winning campaigns
(2016–2026) and Apple's copy evolution. Load this reference when crafting output
at personality levels medium through high.

## Syntactic devices (rhythm architecture)

### Scesis Onomaton (verbless velocity)

Intentional omission of verbs to compress ideas into concrete nouns and
adjectives. Mimics fast-paced internal monologue, forces the reader's brain to
bridge logical gaps.

Formula: `[Concept A]. [Concept B]. [Concept C].`

"Style. Substance. Soul."

Best for: high-density labels, section openers, CL description subject lines.

### Asymmetric tricolon (the pattern breaker)

Three parallel elements where the third breaks the established pattern. The
first two build comfort; the third spikes recall.

Formula: `[Parallel A], [Parallel B], and [Asymmetric C].`

"The beauty you want, the performance you need, and the price you didn't
expect."

Best for: feature summaries, artifact section openers, design rationale
conclusions.

### Asyndeton and polysyndeton (pace sliders)

Gas pedal and brake for reader attention.

-   Asyndeton (omit conjunctions) → rapid, elegant momentum: "Swipe, tap, shop."
-   Polysyndeton (excess conjunctions) → deliberate weight and gravity: "The
    cold and the wind and the miles don't care."

Best for: asyndeton in chat and commit messages (speed); polysyndeton in design
docs when building stakes.

## Semantic devices (memory anchors)

### Antithesis and paradox (cognitive speed bumps)

Sharply contrasting ideas side-by-side. The juxtaposition stops the scrolling
thumb — the brain dedicates a microsecond of active processing to resolve the
contradiction, committing the message to deeper memory.

"The only way to slow down is to move faster."

Best for: artifact headers, CL description rationale, design doc problem
statements.

### Aposiopesis (the open loop)

Intentionally unfinished sentence. Leverages the Zeigarnik Effect — the brain
retains uncompleted loops far more than resolved ones.

"You think you know your limits. Until..."

Best for: design doc problem framing, artifact introductions. Use sparingly — at
most once per document.

### First-person packaging

Elevate the product or system from passive object to active narrator. Removes
corporate distance.

"I'm only 120 calories. Eat me while I'm cold."

Best for: error messages with personality, product descriptions in artifacts.
Requires personality dial at high.

## Platform matrix

Optimal device selection by output context:

Context               | Word budget  | Primary devices
:-------------------- | :----------- | :------------------------------------
CL subject line       | 5-12 words   | Scesis Onomaton, Antithesis
CL body               | 50-200 words | Parallelism, Contrast, Asyndeton
Chat message          | 10-50 words  | Rhetorical questions, Aposiopesis
Design doc / artifact | Unbounded    | Full toolkit, Polysyndeton for stakes
Code comment          | 5-20 words   | None — precision baseline only

## Apple's three eras (study in voice evolution)

### Magisterial formalism (2016-2019)

Monolithic, confident, self-referential.

-   Polyptoton (root repetition): defining a product by its own benchmark. "And
    then there was Pro."
-   Antimetabole (symmetrical reversal): transposing word order across clauses.
    "Large screen. Larger screen."

### Playful conversational paradox (2020-2023)

Colloquial, self-deprecating, rhythmic.

-   Structural antithesis: pairing contrasting benefits to bypass objections.
    "Lots to love. Less to spend."
-   Isolated parataxis: fragments paced by hard punctuation. "Titanium. So
    strong. So light. So Pro."

### Technocentric realism (2024-2026)

Immediate, clear-cut utility. Abstract wordplay replaced with functional
summaries.

-   Metonymy (immediate nominalization): substituting specs with creative nouns.
    "Cinemasterpiece."
-   The dynamic pivot: transitioning features from introductory greetings to
    standard capabilities. "Hello, Apple Intelligence" → "Built for Apple
    Intelligence."
